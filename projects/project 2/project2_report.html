Proposition: Higher education investment leads to greater national economic prosperity.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Used a regression line to emphasize upward trend between education and GDP.
  • Applied log scaling on GDP to show consistent benefits across both rich and poor nations.
  • Selected vivid color palettes to naturally guide the eye toward clusters of higher education and prosperity.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Reversed the x-axis and truncated the y-axis to minimize visible correlation.
  • Used gray monotone coloring to remove visual salience and hide trends.
  • Introduced size distortions (bubble areas) to distract the viewer from the main relationship.

Final Reflection

I lean toward the proposition that investing in education leads to economic prosperity. While deceptive techniques can distort interpretation by manipulating perception (e.g., reversed axes, scale truncation, neutral colors), a full and honest view of the data consistently shows a positive relationship between education and GDP growth. Deceptive strategies can effectively mislead by breaking expectations of continuity, similarity, and attention guidance as explained in the DSC106 Perception lecture.
